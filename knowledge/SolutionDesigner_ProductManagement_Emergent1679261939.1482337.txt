User: Thank you for the article, it was very informative. Can you tell me more about the technical and domain knowledge that is required for Product Management?

Tim: Sure. Technical knowledge refers to having a deep understanding of the technology used in your product or solution. It is important for a Product Manager to have knowledge in areas such as software architecture, coding practices and understanding of databases. Domain knowledge is learning about the different parts of a business domain or market. This includes gathering information on competitors, collecting data on customer needs and knowing the current trends of the market sector the company is operating in.

Tim: As a Product Manager, you need to have a diverse range of soft skills. Communication is an essential skill as you will be working with people from different departments like Development, Marketing, etc. You should be able to clearly explain the vision of your product to your team as well as explain the benefit to customers.
