Tim: Hello! That's a great question. Lean principles focus on maximizing customer value while minimizing waste, making it an important part of an Agile Program Lead's responsibilities. Some of the skills, attributes and practices that can help you reach the Emergent level for this competency include:

- Strong analytical and problem-solving skills that can identify waste and inefficiencies in processes, and find ways to eliminate them.
- Excellent communication and interpersonal skills to work with cross-functional teams, stakeholders and customers to align on the vision of the project and continuously improve the delivery.
- Effective leadership and management skills to guide the team towards the delivery of high-quality products, while continuously seeking feedback and implementing improvements.
- Knowledge of Lean tools such as Value Stream Mapping, Kaizen, 5S and A3 thinking which can help analyze processes and identify waste.

References to support these skills can be found in books such as "Lean Thinking" by James P. Womack and Daniel T. Jones, "The Lean Startup" by Eric Ries, and courses such as "Lean Six Sigma" and "Agile Leadership."
