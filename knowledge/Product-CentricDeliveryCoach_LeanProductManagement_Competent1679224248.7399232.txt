User: Hi Tim, I've been researching the competency requirements for Lean Product Management and wanted to understand what level of skill, attributes, and practices are needed to achieve competency for a Product-Centric Delivery Coach.

Tutor Tim: Sure. In order for a Product-Centric Delivery Coach to be considered competent in Lean Product Management, they need to have a deep understanding of Agile methodologies, Lean thinking principles, and Product Development Frameworks. Additionally, they need to have strong communication, collaboration, and decision-making skills.

User: That makes sense, can you elaborate on the Agile methodologies and Product Development Frameworks?

Tutor Tim: Absolutely. Agile methodologies such as Scrum, Kanban, and Lean Startup are all commonly used in Lean Product Management. Scrum focuses on delivering working software every sprint, while Kanban focuses on visualizing work and limiting work in progress. Lean Startup focuses on iteratively building and testing a product to minimize waste and maximize learning.

For Product Development Frameworks, the most common ones are Lean Product Development, Design Thinking, and Lean UX. All these frameworks help teams validate product assumptions early and often, ultimately leading to more successful products.

User: That's very informative, thank you. Can you recommend any specific literature, articles or journals on this topic?

Tutor Tim: Of course. The Lean Product Playbook by Dan Olsen is an excellent resource. It provides a step-by-step guide to Lean Product Management, with practical tips, case studies, and examples. Another great resource is the book Lean Analytics by Alistair Croll and Benjamin Yoskovitz. This book provides insights into measuring product success and evaluating product market fit in a Lean way.

For academic journals, I recommend the Journal of Product Innovation Management, which covers research on product management, innovation, and entrepreneurship. The International Journal of Strategic Management also covers product management topics.

User: I see. And finally, can you give me an example of how Lean Product Management can be applied in a real-world scenario?

Tutor Tim: Sure. Imagine a startup is building a new social media platform. Through Lean Product Management, the team will first define their target audience and then create a minimum viable product with only the most essential features. They will then conduct user testing, gather feedback, and iteratively improve the product to meet user needs. This helps the team minimize waste, maximize learning and increase the likelihood of success.
