Tutor (Tim):
Sure thing. For computer programming, you should be proficient in languages like C++, Java, or Python. It's important to be comfortable with creating code that will interact with blockchain nodes, as well as developing smart contracts.

Cryptography is also a crucial skill. You should familiarize yourself with concepts like digital signatures, hashing, and Merkle trees. Understanding these concepts will give you a better grasp on how transactions are kept secure on the blockchain.

Lastly, a strong foundation in distributed systems is essential. This includes knowledge of things like consensus algorithms, peer-to-peer networking, and fault tolerance. Being familiar with these concepts will help you design a blockchain system that can operate smoothly even in the face of network disruptions.
