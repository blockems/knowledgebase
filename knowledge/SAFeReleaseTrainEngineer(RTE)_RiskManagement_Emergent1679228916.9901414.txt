Tim: Sure, I recommended the following books, which cover Agile practices, including the SAFe framework:

1. Essential Scrum
2. SAFe 5.0 Distilled: Achieving Business Agility with the Scaled Agile Framework

4. Lean Software Development: An Agile Toolkit by Mary Poppendieck & Tom Poppendieck

These books will give you a thorough understanding of Agile methodologies and the SAFe framework, helping you approach Risk Management in a structured and organized manner.

User: That's helpful, Tim. Can you tell me what kind of soft skills and attributes I need to work on?

Tim: As an RTE, you need to have excellent communication, coordination and leadership skills. You'll be working with multiple teams and stakeholders, and so you should know how to navigate through different personalities and resolve conflicts. Additionally, RTEs should be self-motivated, goal-oriented, and proactive in identifying and mitigating risks that can delay the release train.

References to improve communication and leadership skills include:

1. Crucial Conversations: Tools for Talking When Stakes Are High by Kerry Patterson, Joseph Grenny, Ron McMillan, and Al Switzler
2. The Five Dysfunctions of a Team: A Leadership Fable by Patrick Lencioni
3. Radical Candor: Be a Kick-Ass Boss Without Losing Your Humanity by Kim Scott

User: Thanks again, Tim. Can you recommend any practices or activities that can help improve my understanding and experience with Risk Management in a SAFe environment?

Tim: Definitely. One of the best ways to gain experience is to work in a Risk Management capacity on a SAFe release train. References to improve practices in Risk Management include:
