User: That sounds comprehensive. Can you break it down for me?

Tim: Sure thing. Let's start with skills. An expert Agile coach should have the ability to communicate effectively, facilitate group discussions, coach individuals, and mentor others. They should also be able to deal with conflicts if they arise and have the ability to manage change effectively. 

User: That makes sense. What about attributes?

Tim: Good question. An expert Agile coach should possess a variety of attributes that promote trust, openness, and respect within the team. These include things like emotional intelligence, self-awareness, and the ability to build relationships. 

User: Interesting. How about practices?

Tim: Some key Agile coaching practices include continuous learning, self-reflection, and supporting and encouraging experimentation within the team. They should also have experience with different collaborative techniques, such as feedback loops or retrospectives.

User: And finally, what about areas of knowledge?

Tim: An expert Agile coach should be well-versed in Agile practices and principles, as well as knowledgeable in agile frameworks such as Scrum, Kanban, and Lean. They should also have a good understanding of technical practices that complement Agile, such as continuous integration and deployment.
