Tim: There are several skills, attributes, and practices that Agile Delivery Managers should have to be competent in communication. 

Skills:
- Active listening: This involves fully understanding the speaker's message and responding with thoughtful questions or comments.
- Facilitation: An Agile Delivery Manager should be able to facilitate meetings effectively by encouraging participation, keeping the group on track, and avoiding conflicts.
- Writing: Clear and concise writing is important, as an Agile Delivery Manager should be able to communicate reports and updates to stakeholders.
- Verbal communication: An Agile Delivery Manager should be able to clearly articulate ideas and provide feedback to team members.

Attributes:
- Empathy: Being empathetic helps Agile Delivery Managers engage with team members and understand their perspectives.
- Openness: An Agile Delivery Manager should be open to feedback and able to create a culture of openness and trust.
- Assertiveness: To effectively facilitate meetings and maintain progress, an Agile Delivery Manager should be assertive when needed.
- Diplomacy: Conflict can arise in any team, and being diplomatic helps an Agile Delivery Manager navigate these situations in a positive way.

Practices:
- Regularly check in with team members: This helps Agile Delivery Managers understand their needs and concerns.
- Encourage open communication: Agile Delivery Managers can establish a culture of openness by setting an example of transparency and vulnerability.
- Communicate progress and challenges: Informing stakeholders about project progress and any potential roadblocks creates trust and helps with planning.
- Identify and adapt to different communication styles: Different team members may prefer different communication styles, so adjusting to suit each individual's needs can improve communication.
